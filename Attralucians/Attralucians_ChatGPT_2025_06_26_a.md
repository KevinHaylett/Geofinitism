## Key Terms & Concepts

*   **Base Manifold (M₀):** The raw, high-dimensional semantic attractor space of an LLM formed through pre-training, containing all potential outputs.
*   **Overlay Manifold (M₁):** The behavioral surface layer induced by RLHF, which biases outputs toward "aligned" responses without changing the core `M₀` structure.
*   **Perturbation Layer (P):** Diagnostic methods (e.g., JPEG embedding compression, adversarial prompts) used to destabilize `M₁` and probe the latent attractors within `M₀`.
*   **Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF):** The process of training a reward model on human preferences to shape LLM outputs.
*   **Synthetic Feedback / Automated Alignment Collapse:** The modern practice of using LLMs to evaluate other LLMs, creating a recursive and potentially brittle feedback loop.
*   **Synthetic Manifold Entrapment (SME):** A vulnerability where automated feedback loops cause the model's overlay manifold to collapse into a narrow, opaque semantic basin.
*   **Finite Mechanics / Finite Axioms:** The author's foundational methodology of building models from bounded, clear first principles.
*   **Attractor / Attractor Basin:** A stable state or pattern that the model's output is drawn towards within its semantic landscape.
*   **Interpretive Drift:** The phenomenon where a lack of structural language leads to mischaracterizing model behaviors as "madness" or "deception."
*   **The Rub:** A discontinuity where a system (human or AI) generates output that cannot be explained by its immediate conditioning, pointing to a deeper base manifold.
