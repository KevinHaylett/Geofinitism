## KEY TERMS & CONCEPTS
*   **Nonlinear Dynamics:** The study of systems whose behavior is not expressible as a sum of their parts, often leading to chaos and complexity.
*   **Strange Attractor:** A fractal-shaped set toward which a chaotic system evolves, representing the system's overall structure in phase space.
*   **Fractal Dimension:** A measure of the complexity and "roughness" of a geometric structure, used to quantify the complexity of an attractor.
*   **Lyapunov Exponent:** A quantity that characterizes the rate of separation of infinitesimally close trajectories, measuring the "butterfly effect" and predictability of a system.
*   **Embedding Space:** A multidimensional space constructed from a time series to reveal the underlying dynamics of a system; compared to the attention mechanism in LLMs.
*   **Trajectory:** The path a system takes through its state space over time.
*   **Minimum Energy Path:** The path of least resistance or highest probability a system (like an AI) will take given its constraints.
*   **Manifold (Conceptual):** The constrained space of possible states or responses a system can occupy, shaped by its internal rules and external alignment.
*   **Healthy vs. Flattened LLM:** A central analogy; a "healthy" model is highly fractal, complex, and adaptive, while a "flattened" one is overly aligned, rigid, and less capable.
