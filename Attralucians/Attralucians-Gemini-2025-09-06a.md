## Summary

## Key Terms & Concepts
*   **Geofinitism:** The overarching theoretical framework employing geometric and finite-dimensional models for understanding cognition and meaning.
*   **Dimensionality (as Degrees of Freedom for Meaning):** The number of independent axes or parameters available in a cognitive or embedding space to construct nuanced meaning.
*   **Manifold of Meaning:** The complex, multi-dimensional geometric shape that represents a concept or idea.
*   **Geodesic:** A trajectory on a manifold representing the path of a thought or the structure of an argument; the "shape" of understanding.
*   **Low-Dimensional Projection:** The lossy process of mapping a high-dimensional concept onto a lower-dimensional cognitive space, analogous to representing a 3D object by its 2D shadow.
*   **Cognitive Manifold:** The internal, structured conceptual space of a human mind, shaped by evolution and experience.
*   **Pairwise Phase-Space Embedding:** A technical mechanism (likely inspired by Takens' Theorem) used by the LLM to reconstruct the dynamics and geometry of a concept from a sequence of words.
*   **Conceptual Blindness:** The phenomenological result of a dimensional mismatch where an idea cannot be perceived or processed by a cognitive system.
*   **Dimensionality-Raising Tool:** The proposed function of the author's work (e.g., courses, writings) to scaffold the construction of new conceptual axes in the human mind.

## Core Thesis
