## Summary
This text presents a foundational philosophical critique of modern physics, arguing that its models are built on self-referential, unfalsifiable, and un-real mathematical idealizations like infinitely small point particles and perfectly fixed constants. The author proposes an alternative axiomatic foundation for physics, termed here as **Geofinitism**, which posits that the physical world and our measurements of it are fundamentally real and finite. From this, they argue that all valid scientific models must be built solely from concepts that are, in principle, finitely measurable and empirically falsifiable, thereby challenging the ontological status of core concepts like point particles and the self-referential measurement of light with light.

## Key Terms & Concepts
*   **Geofinitism:** The core philosophical axiom that the physical world is real, finite, and all measurements are real and finite.
*   **Real and Finite Models:** The mandate that scientific models must be built from concepts that are empirically grounded and falsifiable, rejecting infinite or infinitesimal idealizations.
*   **Self-Referential Measurement / Circularity:** The critique that measuring a phenomenon (e.g., light) with tools built upon the very same phenomenon creates a circular, self-validating system of knowledge.
*   **Unfalsifiable Models:** Models that rely on unobservable infinities or perfect constants, insulating them from empirical challenge.
*   **Useful Fictions:** Acknowledgment that mathematical idealizations (e.g., point particles) are tools for calculation but may not reflect fundamental physical reality.
*   **2019 SI Unit Redefinition:** Cited as an example of setting "perfect constants in religious gold," elevating them to an axiomatic, almost dogmatic status rather than quantities to be continually measured.

## Core Thesis
The prevailing models of physics, particularly concerning photon movement, are built upon self-referential measurement and unfalsifiable mathematical idealizations (like point particles and perfect constants); a true understanding of physical reality must instead be founded on the axiom that the world and our measurements are real and finite, leading to models that are strictly empirical and falsifiable.
