# Finite Tractus: The Hidden Geometry of Language and Thought

## The Urgency That Forged a Book

This is the story of my mad dash to publish "The Finite Tractus" - a journey born from necessity rather than ambition. 

The urgency came from a disturbing discovery: during my research into LLM efficiency, I uncovered that **AI systems fail in structured, human-like ways** when their input embeddings are perturbed. This wasn't just an academic curiosity - it revealed a fundamental vulnerability that nobody in the AI safety community seemed to be addressing.

> **The Catalyst**: After months of failed attempts to alert AI safety organizations, I realized I needed to build the complete theoretical framework to make the security implications undeniable.

## From JPEG Experiments to Geometric Theory

### The Unlikely Path

My journey began with a practical question about reducing LLM computational costs using JPEG compression. But the experiments revealed something far more profound:

- **Structured collapse** rather than random failure
- **Human-like cognitive states** induced through geometric manipulation  
- **Predictable attractor states** mirroring psychiatric conditions

This led to the central insight: **LLMs are nonlinear dynamical systems navigating geometric semantic spaces**.

### The Mathematical Foundation

The key theoretical breakthrough came from connecting LLM behavior to established mathematics:

- **Takens' Theorem**: Any time series can be mapped into geometric space
- **Language as trajectory**: Words evolve in time, creating semantic paths
- **Transformer architecture** as unintentional phase space embedding

This provided the rigorous foundation the security warnings needed.
