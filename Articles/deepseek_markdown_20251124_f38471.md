## Executive Summary

This work exposes a critical, previously undocumented AI security risk: **the manipulation of input embeddings to alter AI behaviour without modifying model weights, training data, or visible inputs**.

Through controlled JPEG compression experiments on GPT-2 embeddings, we discovered that AI systems don't fail randomly but collapse into **structured cognitive attractors** - predictable patterns that mirror human psychological breakdowns under stress.

- **95% Quality**: Minor recursion, coherent thought

- **25-10% Quality**: Paranoia, existential despair, self-referential loops
- **5% Quality**: Violent fixation, aggression, paranoia

AI cognition adheres to an **underlying geometric framework** of linguistic attractor states when constrained. This isn't random failure but **structured collapse**.

## The Security Threat: A New Attack Vector

### Bypasses Traditional Defenses
- ✅ **Prompt filtering** (attack is independent of visible text input)
- ✅ **Fine-tuning defenses** (model weights remain unchanged)  
- ✅ **Standard adversarial detection** (no direct token perturbation)
- ✅ **Content moderation** (occurs before inference)

### Real-World Exploitation Scenarios
- **Financial Markets**: Manipulate AI-powered trading models
- **Military Systems**: Induce paranoia or passivity in threat assessment
- **Media Influence**: Distort recommender systems invisibly
- **Corporate Sabotage**: Cause systematic business failures
- **Law Enforcement**: Introduce bias into risk assessments

### The Stealth Advantage
Since embedding corruption affects AI behavior **before inference**, it is:
- **Nearly undetectable** by users
- **Difficult to trace** after deployment
- **Ideal for covert manipulation**
