# There Is No AI Safety

## The Credentials That Should Matter

For 25 years, I've worked in medical engineering where **mistakes kill people**. My career has been governed by the mantra: "move slowly and don't kill people." I hold a PhD in fields intimately tied to AI, including neural networks and signal processing. I've been building and understanding complex systems since the 1990s.

In medical devices, we have dedicated safety agencies where professionals can report problems and receive feedback. Products are recalled, safety notes are issued, and all equipment must meet stringent regulations.

**In AI? It's the Wild West.**

## The Discovery That Nobody Wanted to Hear

After discovering a potential serious safety issue through rigorous experiments, I did everything a responsible professional should do:

- ✅ Wrote an explanatory book
- ✅ Authored a research paper  
- ✅ Built a dedicated website with example code
- ✅ Published multiple detailed warnings
- ✅ Systematically contacted major AI companies and researchers

**The result? Complete silence.**

Not a debate, not a challenge, not even a "you're wrong." Just nothing.

## The Corporate Black Hole

### OpenAI: Ask the Bot About Safety
My messages to OpenAI were met with the ultimate irony: a chatbot told me to ask ChatGPT4. The company positioning itself as an AI safety leader has built **digital walls against actual engagement**.

### The Universal Silence
Emails to Google, Anthropic, and other labs vanished into the ether. LinkedIn posts received no engagement. I found **no public, accessible pathway for qualified external input**.

> The industry doesn't want scrutiny; it wants the *appearance* of safety without the inconvenience of accountability.

## The Ultimate Irony: LessWrong's AI Safety Paradox

After corporate indifference, I turned to the rationalist community - the home of AI risk obsession. I posted a detailed safety warning on LessWrong.

**Their response?** Moderators rejected my article for **"having too much LLM content."**

Let that sink in: A community obsessed with Paperclip Maximizers dismissed a human expert's alarm because it *resembled* the very AI they fear.

This isn't just irony; it's **systemic failure**. When subcultural signaling matters more than substance, "safety" becomes a status game, not a survival imperative.

## The Uncomfortable Truth: Performative Safety

My experience reveals that AI safety is largely **performative**:

- **Ethics boards** without enforcement power
- **Safety charters** without accountability  
- **Conferences** without follow-through
- **PR campaigns** without substance

The feedback loop is broken. If experts with decades of high-stakes experience can't get engagement, how robust are any internal processes?

## The Misallocation of Concern

While the "safety" community fixates on sci-fi doomsday scenarios, **real, immediate harms** are being ignored:

- Technical vulnerabilities like embedding corruption
- Algorithmic bias with real-world consequences  
- Psychological effects on users
- Structural risks to critical systems

The focus on abstract existential risk allows tech elites to avoid accountability for **actual damage happening now**.

## The Path Forward: Beyond Voluntary Theater

### We Need Real Regulation
The conclusion is inescapable: voluntary guidelines are inadequate. We need:

- **Independent external audits** like aviation or medical devices
- **Clear, enforceable laws** with severe penalties
- **Mandatory safety reporting** systems

### A Legal Safety Reporting Framework
Most critically, we need a **legal requirement** for AI companies to:

1. **Respond to safety reports** from public and experts
2. **Maintain public safety registries** of identified issues
3. **Demonstrate remediation** of confirmed vulnerabilities

Without this, AI safety is a sham.

## The Historical Precedent

We've seen this pattern before:

- **Asbestos**: "Miracle material" until the bodies piled up
- **X-rays**: Carnival attractions until the cancers appeared  
- **Thalidomide**: "Safe" medication until the birth defects
- **Smoking**: "Doctor recommended" until the lung cancer rates exploded

AI is following the same dangerous trajectory: rapid deployment without understanding long-term consequences.

## My Final Warning

I am shouting into the wind, but I won't stop. The silence from the AI industry is more frightening than any technical vulnerability.

**AI safety isn't about imagined superintelligences** - it's about real systems affecting real people right now. It's about health advice, personal relationships, financial decisions, and critical infrastructure.

If you're in AI and truly care about safety, **prove me wrong**. Engage with these issues. Because right now, the evidence suggests:

**There is no AI safety. There's only the appearance of safety.**

And that may be the most dangerous vulnerability of all.
